About Me

My journey into cybersecurity started with a spark of curiosity and a Termux terminal. With fingers flying over ZPhisher, I got my first taste of ethical hacking—an electrifying experience that had me hooked. Soon, I discovered Kali Linux, and in the quiet hours of the night, I set it up, diving headfirst into its arsenal of tools.


As a Linux newbie, things weren’t exactly smooth sailing, but persistence paid off. I vividly remember my first attempt at gathering phone number intel—after some research, I landed on PhoneInfoga, only to realize the actual limitations of such tools. But did I stop? Absolutely not. I dabbled in Metasploit, experimented with payload binding, and even wrestled with antivirus bypassing—purely for ethical learning, of course!


Somewhere along this digital adventure, life threw a curveball, and I faced a setback in my admission journey. But fate had its own script, leading me to Daffodil International University, where I’m currently pursuing a degree in Software Engineering. Now, I’m blending my passion for cybersecurity with software development, always exploring, always learning—because curiosity never stops.
